Ballroom dance is an exciting world where grace and rhythm come together to create magic on the dance floor.
Whether you're gliding through a smooth, romantic waltz or adding a playful spin to a lively cha-cha, each step you take in ballroom dance is an invitation to express yourself.

Together, these dances reveal how ballroom is not just about mastering steps—it's about feeling the music, and sharing that with people throughout the world.
Whether you’re new to dancing or an experienced performer, ballroom dance has the power to make every step feel special.

Waltz

History The waltz is one of the oldest and most iconic Ballroom dances. The origins date back to the late 18th century in Austria and Germany. Initially, it was considered scandalous due to its close hold and the spinning movements that set it apart from the more formal dances of the time. The name "waltz" comes from the German word "walzen", meaning "to turn" or "to roll,". It gained popularity in Vienna during the 19th century, especially in aristocratic circles, and by the mid-1800s, it had spread across Europe and to the United States, evolving into various styles such as the Viennese waltz and the slower, more graceful modern waltz we dance today. Known for its elegance, the waltz remains a symbol of timeless beauty, with its flowing, circular steps and steady 3/4 rhythm captivating dancers for centuries.

How to Box turn In the basic waltz box step, the man begins with the left foot, and the woman starts with the right. The pattern is:
1. Step forward with the left foot (man) / Step backward with the right foot (woman).
2. Step to the side with the right foot (man) / Step to the side with the left foot (woman).
3. Step together, bringing feet together with the left foot (man) / Step together, bringing feet together with the right foot (woman).

Princess turn
Man's Role: He will guide the woman with their right hand raised (either at shoulder or head height), leading her into the turn. The leader will step forward (if starting from a box step position) and then pivot slightly to the side, maintaining a strong, guiding hand to support the woman's turn.
Woman's Role: The womans steps into the turn by rotating around the leader’s raised hand. The follower begins by stepping to the side (typically with the left foot), and as she turns, she continues rotating, keeping her feet close together. The rotation is smooth and continuous, with the follower turning 180 degrees or so, completing the turn with a graceful finish.

Traveling Box
Man's Role:
1. Step forward with the left foot.
2. Step sideways to the right with the right foot.
3. Close the left foot to the right foot, completing the first part of the box.
4. Now, step sideways to the left with the left foot, followed by stepping backward with the right foot.
5. Finally, close the left foot to the right foot to complete the full box pattern.
Woman's Role:
1. Step backward with the right foot (mirroring the man's forward movement).
2. Step sideways to the left with the left foot.
3. Close the right foot to the left foot, completing the first part of the box.
4. Now, step sideways to the right with the right foot, followed by stepping forward with the left foot.
5. Finally, close the right foot to the left foot to complete the pattern.

Cha Cha

The "cha-cha" is a lively and flirtatious Latin dance that originated in Cuba in the early 1950s.
The dance’s catchy rhythm, consisting of three quick steps followed by a slower step, quickly caught on in Cuba and was eventually introduced to the United States in the early 1950s.
Its popularity soared after it was shown in Hollywood films and performed in dance competitions.
The cha-cha’s upbeat tempo and playful, flirtatious movements made it an instant hit, and it became a staple in ballroom dance competitions around the world.


How to
Basic

For the Man:
1. Step forward with the left foot (on the first beat of the music, "1").
2. Step backward with the right foot (on the second beat, "2").
3. Step side with the left foot (on the third beat, "3").
4. Step together with the right foot next to the left (this is the "cha-cha-cha" part, on the "and-1" beat).
5. Step backward with the left foot (on the second beat, "2").
6. Step forward with the right foot (on the third beat, "3").
7. Step side with the left foot (on the "and" part, followed by stepping together with the right foot).
For the Woman:
1. Step backward with the right foot (on the first beat, "1").
2. Step forward with the left foot (on the second beat, "2").
3. Step side with the right foot (on the third beat, "3").
4. Step together with the left foot next to the right (on the "and-1" beat).
5. Step forward with the right foot (on the second beat, "2").
6. Step backward with the left foot (on the third beat, "3").
7. Step side with the right foot (on the "and" part, followed by stepping together with the left foot).

CrossOver Breaks

For the Man:
1. Step forward with the left foot (on "1").
2. Step backward with the right foot (on "2").
3. Step side with the left foot (on "3").
4. Step together with the right foot (on the "and" of "cha-cha-cha").
5. Step to the side with the left foot (on "1").
6. Step across with the right foot in front of the left, crossing the feet (on "2").
7. Step side with the left foot (on "3").
8. Step together with the right foot (on the "and" of "cha-cha-cha").
For the Woman:
1. Step backward with the right foot (on "1").
2. Step forward with the left foot (on "2").
3. Step side with the right foot (on "3").
4. Step together with the left foot (on the "and" of "cha-cha-cha").
5. Step to the side with the right foot (on "1").
6. Step across with the left foot in front of the right, crossing the feet (on "2").
7. Step side with the right foot (on "3").
8. Step together with the left foot (on the "and" of "cha-cha-cha").

Butterfly
For the Man:
1. Step forward with the left foot (on the "1").
2. Step backward with the right foot (on the "2").
3. Step side with the left foot (on "3").
4. Step together with the right foot (on "cha-cha-cha").
5. Step side with the left foot, turning slightly to face the follower (on "1").
6. Step to the side with the right foot, bringing your feet apart (on "2").
7. Bring your left foot toward the right foot in a sweeping motion (on "3"), almost as if you're closing your feet to each other, but keeping your weight on the right foot.
8. Step side with the left foot, and then bring the right foot to meet the left, as the feet return to the starting position (on "cha-cha-cha").
For the Woman:
1. Step backward with the right foot (on "1").
2. Step forward with the left foot (on "2").
3. Step side with the right foot (on "3").
4. Step together with the left foot (on "cha-cha-cha").
5. Step side with the right foot, turning slightly to face the leader (on "1").
6. Step to the side with the left foot, opening your legs wide (on "2").
7. Bring your right foot towards the left foot in a sweeping motion (on "3"), while keeping the weight on your left foot.
8. Step side with the right foot, and then bring the left foot to meet the right, returning to the starting position (on "cha-cha-cha").
